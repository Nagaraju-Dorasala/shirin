You’re here for a reason

My guess is we have met in person, someone you trust has mentioned my name or you have seen some of my content in the digital land. However you got here, a warm welcome. 

In this site (and whatever I put out in this world), I have only one and only one intention. To share with you how I have become aware of a challenge in my life, how I have gone about solving it, and what I have learnt in that process.

Sharing that process, helps you collapse the time!

I share my dreams, because I have realised many people have similar dreams or parts of our dreams. That’s how we make friends, that’s how a ripple effect gets started… and then a movement.

My dream is a just and fair world. Everything I do or don’t do is in service of that. I actively look for and welcome new people into my life. People who are futuristic, caring for people and the planet, and driven toward their goals.

Some of:

The companies I have worked for & with

During my 30s-40s, I changed jobs so often and so many different companies, countries and industries that my parents used to think I was getting fired every six months. Sometimes, I worked at two jobs at the same time or consulted on the side. 

They couldn’t comprehend why I’d change so much. My mum is the university’s dean with 35 years of the same office, same faculty, same everything. 

I stopped telling them about my movements.

At work, I was always either the youngest of my peers, because I had the experience level of a 50-year-old professional. Or oldest, because who suffers through learning video editing for social media platforms when “experts” are 20 years younger?

One thing was consistent: I often was the highest-paid person on the table. I was unapologetic about negotiating more, or leaving. 

I wanted to maximise my experience and return on my efforts, and I did.




The Trio -

Writing three books, in one summer.

Longer Story

Ok, I am a bookworm, researcher and collector. But I wasn’t planning to write three books!

I had a Post-it note on my wall for two years with two words: “Your Book”. Anytime I looked at it, I felt I was behind! Everyone I talked to was not encouraging or even supportive. A business coach directly told me “You don’t need a book”

One really early morning, maybe 4 am, I sat, opened a Google doc and started to write!
I clearly remember, I woke up, went to the toilet, went directly to my desk, opened a file and started writing … words were pouring out of my brain.

What about?

My core function, in my hard-core, through-and-through corporate job, is business transformation for profitability and sustainable growth. Basically, I restructure the internal investment and operating model of an organisation. You need strong leadership to make these changes in the organisations. In simple language, you need to summon the force of nature to move things forward.

This is what I’m good at (How humble of me): I started with Book Lead: Lead a Transformation.

When you work with over 1400 senior managers and more than 400 executives, you can see patterns.

You’d know you can’t lead anything if you are not in a high enough position. You are an order taker; you can’t do much. So I wrote the second book, “LAND”. How to Interview and negotiate.

Given my number of jobs, I was qualified for it, LOL.

In the past 10 years, I have not applied for a single job. It was always by referral and by introductions. It wasn’t because of my ability to do the job. There are many other Transformation experts that English is their first language, are male, caucasian and tall, also have a healthy network due to their background and life advantage. (You don’t need me to expand on this any further, do you?)

The next problem I wanted to solve for the reader was: How to become the obvious choice? How do you skip the pain of a job hunt and get referred when most odds are against you?

I wrote Level Up. It is a series of practices and exercises I learnt in years of going through therapy, receiving coaching and mentoring. What is often referred to as “working on yourself”. The stuff that no one openly talks about and is exactly what you need to become a leader.

I self-published Level Up; It became an Amazon best seller in 3 categories. Something that I did not expect. I had to talk to my psychologist to help me accept and absorb the success!

My favourite page of the book is the dedication page!

My philosophy of life

On deeper and more meaningful topics, I have not decided in my next life whether to come back as a philosopher, a healer, or a dog belonging to a rich woman!

I know there is more to life than business ventures. Though I’ve resigned, it is my passion, and I officially own it. I also admit my passion is not the best conversation for the dinner table. I learnt that in a hard way.

One thing I know is that I am clear about my purpose in life: To contribute to creating a more just future for everyone. There is a lot of injustice going on in this world. 

And everything I do, my businesses, my workshops, my interactions … all are in the service of above!

Here are the thoughts I’ve subscribed to 

For beautiful eyes, look for the good in others; for beautiful lips, speak only words of kindness; and for poise, walk with the knowledge that you are never alone. – Audry Hupburn

Never be a spectator of unfairness or stupidity. Seek out argument and disputation for their own sake; the grave will supply plenty of time for silence. -Christopher Hitchens

Elegance is an attitude – Karl Lagerfeld

And now … 

I believe … life is not a marathon; it is a series of sprints – Learn how to define the beginning and end of each.
I believe … in life by design – Very existentialism of me!

And I’ve decided when I die, they will say, I mean, I hope they say, she was kind and generous, did some good stuff and lived her life to the fullest.
